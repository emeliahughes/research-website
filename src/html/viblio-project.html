Viblio: Credibility Signals and Citations on YouTube

As more and more people turn to social media, news aggregators, and search engines as their main source of information, it can become difficult to determine the credibility of the content they see. Media manipulators have learned how to capitalize on the weaknesses of missing information and the current systems in place, driving searchers into primed zones of misinformation and conspiracies. Manipulators do not need to be able to hack into the search engine or change the way the search algorithm works to do this; they simply need to alter the information landscape.

It has been shown that searchers implicitly trust Googleâ€™s search algorithm more than their own judgment (In Google We Trust). The implications of this can easily be seen; an unaware user searches the wrong search terms or clicks on a recommended video and ends up in a new world of misinformation. Currently, credibility signals get lost in the inconsistent formatting of social media and search engines. This leads us to the question:

How can we design and alter information sources so users can easily identify the credibility of a source?
